A Glossary of Talebisms

Words he had to invent.

Coinages that escaped the books and entered the language. Each term links to the volume that introduced it.

Black Swan
A highly improbable event with three properties: it is unpredictable, it carries massive impact — and afterward, we concoct an explanation that makes it seem less random than it was.
From The Black Swan (2007)
Antifragile
Beyond resilient or robust. The resilient resists shocks and stays the same; the antifragile gets better — it gains from disorder, volatility, and stressors.
From Antifragile (2012)
Lindy
For the nonperishable — an idea, a book, a technology — every additional day of survival implies a longer remaining life expectancy. Time is the only honest reviewer.
From Antifragile · developed in the Notebook
Skin in the Game
Symmetry in risk: never trust anyone with the upside of a decision who does not also carry its downside. Advice without exposure is noise.
From Skin in the Game (2018)
Procrustean bed
After the innkeeper who stretched or amputated guests to fit his bed: changing the human to fit the model, instead of the model to fit the human.
From The Bed of Procrustes (2010)
IYI — Intellectual Yet Idiot
The credentialed semi-erudite who tells others what to do without ever bearing the consequences of being wrong.
From Skin in the Game (2018)

The Bio · “No fluffy stuff, no honors, ranks, etc.”

Twenty-one years a risk taker. Then the ideas.

Nassim Nicholas Taleb spent 21 years as a quantitative trader — closing 650,000 option transactions and examining 200,000 risk reports — before changing careers in 2006–2010 to become a scholar, mathematical researcher, and philosophical essayist. He “traveled the conventional route in inverse sequence — from the practical to the philosophical to the mathematical.”

Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering at NYU Tandon (retired). Scientific advisor at Universa Investments. He refuses all honors and anything that “turns knowledge into a spectator sport.”

“Flaneur: probability (philosophy), probability (mathematics), probability (real life), Phoenician wine, deadlifts & dead languages. Greco-Levantine. Canaan.” — his own bio, on X

“The book was not submitted to any reviewer. There was no U.S. book tour, no promotion, no advance copies, just a single lecture in NYC. Yet it mysteriously opened at #2 on the NYT bestsellers list.” — on Skin in the Game, from his site
